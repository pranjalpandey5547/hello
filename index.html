Process

7 Phases to Build Your System

From planning to feeding — the complete step-by-step guide.

1
📋

Design & Planning

Define scope, select site, plan infrastructure.

  • Estimate daily waste volume
  • Choose target animals
  • Assess site location
  • Plan water, power, drainage
2
🪣

Collection

Install color-coded bins at source points.

  • Green bins for animal-safe scraps
  • Red bins for hazardous waste
  • Daily or alternate-day pickup
  • Refrigerated transport at scale
3
🏭

Processing Facility

Build receiving, sorting, and processing areas.

  • Concrete floor with drains
  • Stainless steel sorting table
  • Steam cooker or fermenters
  • Weighing and logging station
4
💧

Wastewater System

Manage liquid waste and odors.

  • Grease trap at all drains
  • Settling + biogas digester
  • Biofilters for odor control
  • Windbreak tree perimeter
5
🐄

Feeding Infrastructure

Build appropriate feeding stations.

  • Stainless steel pig troughs
  • Wet feeding pump systems
  • Hanging poultry feeders
  • Aquaculture auto-dispensers
6
📊

Monitoring

Track inputs, outputs, and animal health.

  • Weighing systems at entry/exit
  • Temperature loggers
  • pH meters for fermentation
  • CCTV security monitoring
7
✅

Compliance

Register with authorities and meet biosecurity rules.

  • Register with agriculture dept.
  • Follow biosecurity protocols
  • Quarterly lab pathogen tests
  • Veterinary oversight

Processing

3 Processing Methods

Choose based on animal type, scale, and budget.

🔥

Heat Treatment

The most common and legally required method for pig swill.

Requirements100°C for min. 60 minutes. Industrial steam cooker. Temperature logging required.
🧪

Fermentation (Bokashi)

Lacto-fermentation using Effective Microorganisms improves digestibility.

RequirementsSealed tanks (200–1000L). EM inoculant. pH kit. 7–14 day process.
🌬️

Drying & Milling

Converts waste into shelf-stable nutrient-dense feed pellets.

RequirementsIndustrial dryer (<15% moisture). Hammer mill. Optional pelletizer.
Safety & Biosecurity

Keep Animals Safe

Follow these protocols to prevent disease and keep your herd healthy.

🌡️
Heat Treatment Mandatory

Cook pig swill to 100°C for at least 60 minutes. Temperature logging required in most countries.

🔬
Quarterly Lab Testing

Test processed feed every 3 months for Salmonella, E. coli, and African Swine Fever virus.

👨‍⚕️
Veterinary Oversight

Have a licensed vet perform regular health checks on animals receiving waste-based feed.

✅ Safe to Feed
  • Vegetable peels
  • Fruit scraps
  • Cooked grains & rice
  • Stale bread (not moldy)
  • Dairy (limited)
  • Eggshells (chickens)
  • Coffee grounds
❌ Never Feed
  • Onions & garlic
  • Avocado
  • Chocolate
  • Moldy food
  • Raw meat (most animals)
  • Salty/spicy foods
  • Alcohol or caffeine

Regulatory

Compliance Checklist

Meet all local and national requirements before going live.

🏛️
Register with Authorities

Register with your local agriculture department before collecting or processing waste.

🛡️
Biosecurity Protocols

Especially critical for pig swill due to Foot and Mouth and African Swine Fever risks.

Lab Testing (Quarterly)

Submit processed feed for pathogen testing every quarter. Keep records for 3+ years.

Daily Record Keeping

Log all incoming waste: source, weight, processing method, temperature, and animals fed.
